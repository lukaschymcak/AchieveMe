# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary audience spans casual PC gamers who use Steam emulators or offline saves and hardcore achievement completionists. Both open AchieveMe to answer the same question: where do I stand across every game and emulator source? A connected website for sharing and social features is planned as a secondary surface; the desktop app remains the source of truth for progress data.

## Product Purpose

AchieveMe unifies achievement progress from Goldberg, GSE, CODEX, RUNE, Hoodlum, CreamAPI, and Reloaded save folders into one local library, enriched with Steam Web API metadata. The primary job is at-a-glance progress: completion percentages, recent unlocks, and what's left per game. Backup and restore (export/import ZIPs) is the secondary job — keeping emulator saves portable and safe across machines. Success means progress feels share-worthy: users should want to show off tiers, rings, and completion, setting up a future web layer for social sharing without redesigning the core data model.

## Positioning

The one place to see emulator achievement progress — regardless of which crack or emulator wrote the save — with Steam-quality metadata and a showcase-ready presentation of what you've earned.

## Brand Personality

Steam-native familiarity meets trophy-case pride. Dark, familiar chrome that feels at home beside the Steam client, with celebratory unlock presentation borrowed from PlayStation trophies: tier metals, completion rings, and a showcase energy that makes progress worth displaying. Confident and satisfying without gambling flash or spreadsheet austerity.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard patterns: cream backgrounds, identical card grids, eyebrow kickers, hero-metric templates
- Gambling and RGB-neon aesthetics: flashy gradients, loot-box energy, overstimulating motion
- Bare spreadsheet utility: raw tables with zero personality and no earned celebration

## Design Principles

- **Progress is the product** — every screen answers where the user stands: completion, recent unlocks, and what's left.
- **Earn the celebration** — unlock moments and tier presentation should feel showcase-worthy, not noisy or gamified.
- **Familiar shell, distinctive trophies** — dark Steam-adjacent chrome with PlayStation-inspired tier pride and completion rings.
- **Set up once, stay current** — the library reflects disk state through watchers and refresh without manual babysitting.
- **Share-ready by design** — layout, stats, and visual hierarchy should structure naturally for a future connected website without a ground-up rethink.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline: sufficient contrast on the dark theme (especially body text and placeholders), full keyboard navigation across library, detail, and settings flows, and reduced-motion alternatives for progress-bar fills, delete pulses, and card hover effects.
